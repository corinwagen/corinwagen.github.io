Corin Wagen

About

I'm interested in problems at the interface of organic, computational, and analytical chemistry, with an emphasis on developing computational techniques that enable practical advances.

On a more abstract level, I like thinking critically about scientific institutions, progress, and governance. (I'm also part of the inaugural class of UATX Polaris Fellows.) I write about these issues on my blog, which you can also follow on Substack.

From 2019 to 2023, I was a graduate student in the Jacobsen group at Harvard, where I studied the development and mechanism of selective organocatalytic reactions. My thesis defense can be viewed on Youtube.

From 2016 to 2019, I was an undergraduate at MIT, where I majored in chemistry and studied the design of new phosphine ligands for Pd-catalyzed C–F coupling in the Buchwald group.
