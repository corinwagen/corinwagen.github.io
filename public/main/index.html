Corin Wagen

About

I'm the co-founder and CEO of Rowan. We're building a modern computational toolbox for drug design and materials science.

Rowan uses physics and ML to power high-level scientific workflows that give researchers insights and saves them time in the lab. We also make it easy to use computation by providing a web-based no-code platform and a RDKit/Python-native API, both of which you can try for free. If you're interested in using Rowan (or helping to build it), let me know!

Apart from Rowan, I like thinking critically about scientific institutions, progress, theology, and books. I write about these things on my blog, which you can also follow on Substack.

From 2019 to 2023, I was a graduate student in the Jacobsen group at Harvard, where I studied the development and mechanism of selective organocatalytic reactions. My thesis defense can be viewed on Youtube. (I was also part of the inaugural class of Polaris Fellows at the University of Austin.)

From 2016 to 2019, I was an undergraduate at MIT, where I majored in chemistry and studied the design of new phosphine ligands for Pd-catalyzed C–F coupling in the Buchwald group.

Writing

I write about a variety of topics on this blog. Here's a few suggested places to start, by topic.

Chemistry

By training and occupation, I'm a chemist, and much of what I write is chemistry-adjacent. Here's a few examples:

  • Long-Range Forces and Neural Network Potentials, a review of different NNP architectures and how they handle electrostatic interactions.
  • Teach Yourself Quantum Chemistry For Fun And Profit, which recounts all the papers I found helpful in learning how density-functional theory worked.
  • What Achilles Said to the Tortoise about Binding-Affinity Prediction, a Douglas Hofstadter-style dialogue about using ML to predict protein–ligand interactions.
  • Against Carbon NMR, where I argue that journals shouldn't require 13C NMR for routine characterization. This post was not well-received.

Scientific Advice

I've written a fair amount of advice for scientists and people in scientific fields:

  • You Should Read The Literature More, which encourages scientists to spend more time reading papers (or blogs).
  • Opinionated Advice for Incoming Graduate Students, the advice that I would give to a new chemistry graduate student.
  • How To Give An External Research Talk, adapted from internal Rowan advice that I gave to our interns.

Metascience

Sometimes I write about scientific institutions and how they can be improved:

  • Do We Still Need Journals?, in which I argue that the modern scientific journal is not long for this world and speculate about what might replace it.
  • Creative Software Licenses, a sarcastic look at the now-common practice of releasing ML models under academic-only non-open-source licenses.
  • For-Profit Micro Focused Research Organizations: A Proposal, which argues that for-profit startups can fix some of the problems with the modern applied-research landscape.

Religion and History

And, for fun, I also write about Christianity, medieval history, and whatever else I'm thinking about:

  • My review of Montaillou, where I examine popular misconceptions about the daily lives of medieval peasants.
  • The Ruthless Elimination of Mottes, a critical review of John Mark Comer's The Ruthless Elimination of Hurry.
  • What Did The Early Church Think About Fasting?, in which I do a "patristic lit review" to understand the tangled mess of church tradition around fasting.
